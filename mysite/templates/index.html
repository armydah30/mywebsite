PORTFOLIO

MonsieurMeteo

I am currently working on a research project in developing a weather information system called MonsieurMeteo for farmers in rural Africa. The farmers in rural Africa have little to no access to internet, so they are unable to simply check the internet to get weather information that could greatly impact their productivity. The aim of the project is to therefore discover how reliable weather information can be made instantly available in rural areas in Africa where there is little to no access to internet or smart phones. Therefore, for this project I am making use of technologies like Python, VXML, Django, JSON APIs, Data Analysis and Linked Data. The use of JSON APIs is to get weather forecasts predictions that are reliable and integrate them to the system. While the use of VXML is to build a voice application that will serve as a medium for the farmers to be able to access the system and get weather information via their simple mobile phones. My idea is to make use of the current technology readily available in rural Africa, and build a solution around it, hence, the integration of a voice application that readily allows farmers get weather information through their simple GSM phones. However, the system will not only provide weather forecasts, it will provide the farmers with tips and advisory information on what farming activities can be carried out in response to the upcoming weather. This requires me to work with data and build a knowledge base that will be integrated with the system to provide more services to the farmer. For this project I’ve carried out a lot of research, and have been able to improve and develop new skills.

eCitiBiz

While working at the company Anchor Dataware Solutions, I worked on developing an online and mobile application for the government, to be used by millions of people in Nigeria. The application involved developing an integrated web application, for marriage registrations, citizenship applications, and foreign business registrations/expatriate work permit applications. For this project, I designed all aspects of the system, which involved defining all requirements for the system, designing the system logic, UI, and database structure. eCitiBiz is a robust system that is able to hold: the national marriage records of all marriages in Nigeria, all citizenship application records, and thousands of company records. Each individual and company has an account on the system where they are able to apply for multiple service online. The system integrates a backend interface that provides the agents at the ministry with a platform to work and process all applications online. I made designs of the system from scratch, gathering requirements from the agents at the ministry, understanding the workflow and processes, then transforming them into technical requirements for the system. All processes for around 30 different application types were completely mapped and captured in the system, making it possible for the Ministry agent to be able to carry out all their activities digitally and online. I worked as the project lead in the development of this system and ensured that the system met all functional and logical requirements. I conducted and supervised all necessary tests for the system to weed out errors and ensure the system meets all necessary requirements.

Case Tracking System

I designed a Case Tracking System for the Nigerian government agency called PTAD. The app was developed to help a department of the agency keep a record of all cases that were submitted and needed to be handled. The system involved replicating most of the processes being carried out in the department, while eliminating redundant or ineffective ones. The system automated the way the case handling processes were being carried out, and allowed the department to track the progress of cases from one personnel to another within the department. I worked on designing the system functionalities, architecture and database structure. I managed a small team of three developers and we worked on developing the system to meet the set deadline and requirements. I led a short training that was organized in order to show the agents in the department how to use the system and how they can perform each of their roles with the application. The system is currently hosted internally, and has been able to help the department carry out its duties more efficiently and with more accountability.
